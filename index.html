Need of Help!


Excessive rains since May have caused the worst flooding Bangladesh has seen in over two decades. Bangladesh has deployed its army to the impacted eastern areas of Sylhet, Sunamganj and Kurigram after water levels rose drastically, submerging houses and causing people to take shelter on roofs. On June 17th, 2022 all lines of communication in the region were destroyed, leaving nearly 500,000 people in 600 villages cut off from the rest of the country and stranded without any sources of food or drinking water.


Donate Now


Recurrent flooding, cyclones, and storm-surges are common events in Bangladesh that put millions of children at risk. Every year they leave vast devastation and damage in these areas, and we need to be ready to act.


Our Donation Plan


When a disaster strikes, your donation will make a difference. Please help support UNICEF to ensure we are ready before and when disasters happen.


  • Food for Family


    Your donations can help us to support children affected by undernutrition, making sure that therapeutic milk is available at the nutrition facilities and ready for distribution.


  • Shelter for Family


    Your donations can protect children and families against waterborne diseases through the immediate supply of hygiene kits, jerrycan and water purification tablets that provide immediate relief on the ground.


  • Education of a child


    Your donations also help us support continued education for children during and after floods and cyclones, providing learning support via makeshift schools, and distributing emergency learning kits.
